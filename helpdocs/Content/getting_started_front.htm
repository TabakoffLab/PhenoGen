Overview

The PhenoGen website shares experimental data within the INIA West Consortium, as well as a worldwide community of investigators and provides a flexible, integrated, multi-resolution repository of neuroscience data, ranging from molecules to behavior, for collaborative research on alcoholism.

In addition to providing a comprehensive system to organize, query, analyze, and retrieve high-throughput gene expression data, the website provides the Consortium members with computational tools for integrated analysis of neuroscience data, biomedical literature, gene functional annotations, and Quantitative Trait Loci (QTLs).

The PhenoGen website allows you to:

  • Upload microarray raw data into a MIAME-compliant database.
  • Upload gene lists.
  • Share data with other INIA investigators.
  • Search literature.
  • Translate gene identifiers to and from multiple databases.
  • Access databases containing behavioral QTL data.
  • Match physical location of genes of interest and their eQTL to behavioral QTLs.
  • Correlate gene expression with a phenotype using multiple data sets.

You can also perform:

  • Microarray data quality control analysis and normalization.
  • Data filtering (noise filtering).
  • Statistical analyses, including most common statistical tests and permutations.
  • Promoter analysis (Transcription factors).
  • Obtain information about genetic variations (e.g. SNPs or polymorphisms) in the transcripts of interest.

Before You Begin

Before you can use the PhenoGen website, you must register and set up a user profile. Your user profile provides details such as your name, email address, and the Principal Investigator you are working with. Your user profile can be modified at any time when you are logged into the website. After you complete your registration, you can use the website.

Registration

Required fields on the Registration page are denoted with an asterisk *.

  1. Select your Title and enter your First Name, Middle Initial, and Last Name.
  2. Enter a Username. If the username you enter is the same as an existing name, an error message displays when you try to register.
  3. Enter a Password, then re-enter the password. For security, a password should include numbers and upper and lower case letters.
    Note:     Your Username and Password are used to log in to the website.
  4. Enter your contact Email, Phone, and Fax.
  5. Enter the Name of your institution, your Department, and your Box number.
  6. Enter your Address.
  7. Click Register. Your request for registration is sent. You can also click Reset to clear all the fields.

Logging into the Website

On the Start page that displays at http://phenogen.ucdenver.edu:

  1. Enter your Username.
  2. Enter your Password.
  3. Click the Login button below the Password field to log in.
