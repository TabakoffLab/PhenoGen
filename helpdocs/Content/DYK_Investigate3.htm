You can find genes with trans eQTLs within your pQTL region?

If the physical location of a gene is near the location of transcriptional control, the gene is considered to be cis-regulated. Otherwise, the gene is considered to be trans-regulated. Both types of regulation can be identified with the use of the chromosomal map on the Location and eQTL page.

  1. Access the chromosomal map view of your genes from the Location and eQTL page of the Investigating QTL Regions tab.
  2. Choose Show physical location of genes and Show locations of transcription control in brain in the Gene Graphic Settings section to display the green and red markers.
  3. Choose Advanced Settings, select or define a new region of interest (i.e., pQTL region; see Entering Phenotypic QTLs to set up these regions).
  4. Choose Transcription control (eQTL) within region in the Configure Graphic by Region of Interest section to identify the eQTLs (green markers) in your pQTL region.
  5. Download this gene list for viewing.
  6. Choose No restrictions  in the Configure Graphic by Region of Interest section. Scan the physical locations of genes (red markers) for genes that are regulated by your genes in your downloaded gene list, but are not in the pQTL region.
