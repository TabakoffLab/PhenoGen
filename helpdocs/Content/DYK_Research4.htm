You can reduce the number of genes in your list by their physical location or the location of their eQTL?

You can eliminate some of the genes on your gene list that are not physically located in specific user-defined regions (e.g., pQTL regions; see Entering Phenotypic QTLs to set up these regions).

  1. Access the chromosomal map view of your genes and user-defined regions from the Location tab of the Research Genes tab.
  2. Choose Advanced Settings, select your region of interest, then choose Physical location within region in the Configure Graphic by Region of Interest section. The gene list you download from this selection is limited to those genes in the physical location of your region of interest.

Another way to eliminate some of the genes in your gene list is to exclude the eQTLs (green markers on chromosomal map) that do not lie in the region of interest.

  1. Access the chromosomal map view of your genes and user-defined regions from the Location tab of the Research Genes tab.
  2. Choose Show locations of transcription control in brain in the Gene Graphic Settings section to display the green markers.
  3. Choose Advanced Settings, select your region of interest, then choose Transcription control (eQTL) within region in the Configure Graphic by Region of Interest section. The gene list you download from this selection is limited to those eQTLs in your region of interest.
